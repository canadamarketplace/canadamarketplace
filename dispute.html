Dispute Resolution Policy

Last Updated: January 2025 · Version 1.0 · Canada Marketplace Inc.

1. Overview

We encourage buyers and sellers to communicate through our messaging system to resolve issues first. If that fails, our formal dispute process ensures fair outcomes for both parties.

2. Valid Dispute Reasons

  • Item not received (no tracking or delivery confirmation after 14 days from shipping)
  • Item significantly not as described (different from listing photos, description, or condition)
  • Item arrived damaged (photos required)
  • Wrong item received
  • Suspected counterfeit or fake item

Not valid: "I changed my mind," "It doesn't fit," "I found it cheaper elsewhere."

3. Filing Timeline

Buyers must file a dispute within 14 days of the order status changing to "Delivered." After 14 days, the dispute window closes permanently.

4. Process

  1. Day 0 — Dispute Filed: Buyer submits dispute with reason, description, and photo evidence. Escrow funds are frozen.
  2. Day 1-3 — Seller Response: Seller is notified and has 3 days to respond with their evidence (photos, tracking, messages).
  3. Day 3-7 — Review: Canada Marketplace reviews all evidence from both parties.
  4. Day 7-10 — Decision: We issue a decision: full refund to buyer, partial refund, or release funds to seller.
  5. Day 10 — Resolution: Decision is executed. Funds are released or refunded accordingly. Both parties are notified.

5. Evidence Guidelines

Strong evidence includes: clear photos of the item received, screenshots of the listing vs. what arrived, shipping tracking showing delivery, and message history. Weak evidence: verbal claims without photos, delayed reports.

6. Possible Outcomes

OutcomeBuyer GetsSeller Gets
Full Refund100% refund to original payment methodNo payout, item not returned
Partial RefundPercentage refund (e.g., 50%)Reduced payout
Release to SellerNo refundFull payout minus 8% fee

7. Appeals

Either party may appeal within 7 days of the decision by submitting new evidence that was not available during the original review. Appeals are reviewed by a different team member. The appeal decision is final.

8. Limitations

Canada Marketplace is not a court of law. Our decisions are based on the evidence provided. For disputes involving amounts over $5,000 CAD, you may wish to seek independent legal advice.
